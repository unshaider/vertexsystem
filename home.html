Types of Viruses

SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19):

This coronavirus caused the ongoing global pandemic, leading to widespread illness, death, economic disruption, and significant healthcare challenges.

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus):

The virus that causes AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome), impacting the immune system and leading to severe health complications.

Influenza Virus (Flu):

Seasonal influenza viruses, such as H1N1 or H3N2 strains, cause yearly outbreaks resulting in respiratory illness worldwide.

Hepatitis B Virus (HBV):

A significant cause of hepatitis, leading to liver inflammation, chronic infection, and potentially long-term liver problems.

Hepatitis C Virus (HCV):

Another major cause of hepatitis, often leading to chronic liver disease and complications if left untreated.

Ebola Virus:

Known for causing severe and often fatal hemorrhagic fever outbreaks in Africa, with high mortality rates during certain outbreaks.

Zika Virus:

Linked to birth defects when contracted by pregnant women and known to cause neurological complications in some cases.

Varicella-Zoster Virus (VZV):

Causes chickenpox during initial infection and can later reactivate as shingles, a painful rash.

Human Papillomavirus (HPV):

A common sexually transmitted infection leading to various health issues, including cervical cancer and genital warts.

Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV):

Causes oral and genital herpes, leading to recurring outbreaks of sores or blisters.

Rotavirus:

A leading cause of severe diarrhea, particularly in infants and young children, which can result in dehydration and hospitalization.

Measles Virus:

Highly contagious and can cause severe complications, including pneumonia, encephalitis, and in some cases, death.
